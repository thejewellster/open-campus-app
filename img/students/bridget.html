What is Open Campus?

As college students search for real-world experience to gain leverage in the job market, businesses are also looking for help to complete projects or tasks. When these two intersect, the internship is typically the only option, but internships are not the answer anymore, especially unpaid ones. Open Campus replaces internships with student freelance work. Jobs posted on Open Campus are skill-oriented projects that range from 1 day to 3 months. By categorizing jobs in this fashion, businesses gain access to students who have the skills and time availability to the job done that might otherwise remain incomplete, and students build résumés and gain practical experience. Everyone wins with Open Campus.

"Opening a restaurant"

Running a restaurant is a 24/7 job. You’re busy focusing on doing what you do best: serving patrons. But what bring patrons in the door in the first place? For that, you need marketing and a little bit of innovation, and you don’t need a flying drone to wow the customers.

1. Powerful menu design

At the end of the day, menues are your salespeople. They’ll upsell that bottle of water, close deals on a New York Steak, push the martini, and finishing combo of coffee with dessert. In fact, a well crafted menu is proven to boost restaurant traffic by 20%.

2. Enticing food photography

No matter the culture, language, or age, everyone understand a photograph. An enticing visual of food encourages patrons to order. Get customers excited for their meal.

3. Effective Business Cards

Innovation is key here. You need to stand out from the rest, just like your restaurant. A great design will a piece of paper and turn it into a pocket advertisement

4. High-quality brochure design

Brochures make the sale for you. Images of food intrigue. Your best dishes catch eyes and hungry stomachs alike while a coupon appeals to the wallet.

5. Functional Website

A restaurant’s website needs to be easy to navigate with quality design that capture your restaurants brand in one look. Knowledge of basic directions for functional website creation Check out these tasty designs: http://www.awwwards.com/tasty-design-restaurant-and-catering-websites.html. Create a vision for how you want to portray your website then pass the job off to a web designer and web developer to make it come alive.

7. Restaurant Logo Design

A unique logo design needs to match the concept of your restaurant. A skilled graphic designer will craft a logo that keeps patrons hungry.

8. Interior Design

The design of your restaurant is your chance to show off the brand you have created. Display that logo! Outdoors, indoors, in the menus, above the bar, and at the point of sell. Decor to match the concept provides immediate exposure for patrons. Give them something to remember.

"Starting my own business"

In an early-stage startup, chances are there are skill gaps present between projects that need to get done and the limited staff on hand. You need to fill this knowledge gap, and on a day to day project basis, you need not look further than the local college. Open Campus provides access to talented students who are eager to get involved in your startup.

1. Fully Responsive Website

You need a website in order to be taken seriously. You need an online presence for supporters, employees, patrons, and more trust your organization. A website provides credibility and an important first impression. Gain access to countless web designers and developers on Open Campus and improve your online presence immediately.

2. CFO

Financial projections may seem like guesswork at times, but having detailed work shows investors you’ve done your homework. Your business is creating a product, not financials. Outsource this work and let those with a finance education take the reigns.

3. Market Researchers

Truly successful startups focus on the “why,” not the “what.” Startups that seek to continuously learn from their market are the first to succeed. Market research helps companies stream information that tells you why people buy your product or use your services. Hiring someone else to do your market research can save you time and uncover information you may have overlooked. Open Campus gives access to those with the skills to help you go from product to market.

4. Marketing Video

Video and photography engages customers beyond text and photos alone. Video shows faces, voices, and the personality of a company, which enhances marketing efforts on any channel. Video production and photography is so easy to do because of simple software and functional gear from professional quality gear to smartphone cameras. Need some ideas? Open Campus talent can help create video and photo product demos, customer testimonials, donation drives, and tours of the company.

5. Blog Content

Whether you’re taking your blog or other publication up a notch or starting from scratch, content is key. You need web design and content that captures an audience. Use Open Campus to find a students with these exact skill sets to help you get the job done on your organization’s blog and website content.
